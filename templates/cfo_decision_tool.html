Section 1 · Three Variables That Drive the Number

What Frank's Face-Validity Lens Adds to the Math

Variable 1 · Episode Character
Standard vs Heterogeneous

Standard episodes (CABG, Valve, PCI, Thyroidectomy) have tight procedure lists and diagnoses that aren't the driver. When a facility runs at O:E > 1.10, it's real — acuity not captured by risk-adjustment or genuine complication-driven excess. ~70% addressable.

Heterogeneous episodes (EGD, Mastectomy, Colectomy, Bariatric, Cholecystectomy, Ventral Hernia, Leg Amp) bundle different procedures or diagnoses. Some "outlier" status reflects case-mix or definitional variance, not facility performance. ~50% addressable.

Variable 2 · Facility Persistence
Episode-Specific vs Systemic Outlier

If a facility is an outlier in one episode, the gap is likely episode-specific — engage on that episode's drivers. If a facility is an outlier across multiple episodes, the pattern points to systemic waste, complication management, or care-coordination gaps. These facilities are the highest-leverage targets for both referral steering and QI engagement — the model surfaces them in Section 4.

Section 2 · The Levers

Two Primary Levers, Honestly Bounded

Lever A — Primary
Informed Referral Steering (RAE + PCP)

RAEs and primary care providers, using published facility-level episode performance, steer referrals away from high-O:E facilities toward lower-O:E facilities for elective procedural episodes. Mechanically a case-mix shift; no facility takes a rate cut. Most powerful on elective episodes with concentrated outliers. Does not apply to OB, emergent, or non-elective episodes.

Lever C — Primary
Outlier-Targeted QI Engagement

Voluntary engagement with facilities at O:E > 1.10 on the specific drivers of their excess — complications, readmissions, ED visits within the episode window, redundant post-procedural services. The Episode team supplies analytics, peer benchmarks, and improvement frameworks; the facility owns the change. Collaborative QI — no withholds, no penalties, no shared-savings contracts.

Explicitly out of scope. This model assumes no fee-schedule rate cuts, no payment withholds, no mandatory upside/downside risk, no shared-savings settlement, and no "performance recovery" cycle. Those mechanisms are not available to the CFO and are not relied on here. Public Transparency and Appropriateness / Site-of-Service shift are real but small in this population — named, not modeled.

Section 7 · Defensibility & Team Function

Why the Numbers Hold Up, What the Model Doesn't Know

Outlier excess is measured, not assumed

The $@@TOTAL_OUTLIER_EXCESS_M@@M figure is the sum of (actual − expected) across the @@N_OUTLIERS@@ facility-episode combinations at O:E > @@OE_THRESHOLD@@ in the Colorado APCD risk-adjusted cost output, with a vol ≥ @@MIN_VOLUME@@ filter applied to suppress small-n noise. It is not a calibration to published programs — it is Colorado's own number. Adjustments to lever intensity move what share of $@@TOTAL_OUTLIER_EXCESS_M@@M is recovered, not the ceiling itself.

Classification governs addressability

Frank's face-validity framework: Standard episodes (tight procedure list, diagnosis not the driver) have outliers that are real performance gaps — 70% addressable. Heterogeneous episodes (mixed procedures or diagnoses lumped into one episode definition) have outliers partly explained by definitional variance — 50% addressable. Both defaults are overrideable per episode in Section 3. The case-mix finding in Section 5 (identical 32.9% high-risk volume in outliers vs non-outliers) confirms the risk-adjustment is not the bottleneck.

The team engages targets, not statewide averages

The top-N selector in the controls panel reflects the team's operational reality: a 3–6 FTE team does not touch @@N_OUTLIERS@@ facility-episodes. It engages a focused set. The current setting (Top 20) captures @@TOP_20_PCT_INT@@% of the $@@TOTAL_OUTLIER_EXCESS_M@@M ceiling — and that's where the model's savings live, scaled by the addressability and lever intensity.

Non-overlap math

Lever A and Lever C cannot both claim the same dollar. When volume migrates under Lever A on an elective episode, that volume leaves the outlier facility and is no longer available for Lever C engagement. The model multiplies Lever C savings by (1 − migration_rate) on elective episodes to prevent double-counting. On non-elective episodes (OB, emergent), Lever A does not apply.

How the Team Operates the Levers (From Proof to Program → This Model)

Lever A — Referral Steering is delivered by the Stakeholder Engagement Lead (RAE relationship + PCP communication) and the Data & Analytics Lead (facility-level episode performance reports, refreshed quarterly).

Lever C — QI Engagement is delivered by the Implementation Coordinator (90-day QI cycles at top-N targets) and the Clinical Lead (complication and post-procedural service definitions, peer-benchmark logic).

Target list ownership — systemic outliers and top-N facility-episodes — sits with the Director, who holds the engagement decisions and the stakeholder relationships those decisions require.
